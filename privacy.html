We use a variety of tracking technologies on our Site and App:

  • Cookies: We may use cookies to identify you and enhance your experience on the Site. Cookies are small text files stored on your device to support functionality. While security measures are in place, we are not responsible for data breaches beyond our control over the Internet. You may disable cookies in your browser, but this may limit certain features. Where cookies collect personal data, it will be used for stated purposes. By using the Site, you consent to our use of cookies.
  • Web Beacons: Web beacons are small graphic images on a website or in an email that can be used for such things as recording the pages and advertisements clicked on by users, or tracking the performance of email marketing campaigns.
  • Analytics Tags: We use analytical tags to analyse what you like to do and the effectiveness of our features. They can also help us customize your browsing experience. We may use information collected through analytical tags or tracked links in combination with your Personal Data you provide to us with other Personal Data such as purchase history and demographic information.
  • Web Server Logs: Web server logs are records of activity created by the mobile device or computer that delivers the webpages you request to your browser. The Web server log also may record information about your browser, such as your IP address and the cookies set on your browser by the server.
  • Geo-Location Technologies: We may use geo-location technology to determine your location, either from information you provide (e.g., postal code) or via your device. You can allow or deny location access when prompted. If enabled, we may use this data to send location-based offers. You can opt out anytime through your device settings.
  • QR Codes: QR code consists of black squares arranged in a square grid on a white background which can be read by an imaging device such as a camera. QR code is a machine-readable optical label that contains information about the item to which it is attached. QR codes often contain data for a locator, identifier, or tracker that points to a website or application. Application include product tracking, item identification, time tracking, document management, and general marketing.
